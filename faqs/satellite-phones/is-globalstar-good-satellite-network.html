Is Globalstar a good satellite phone network?

Globalstar has significant coverage gaps and reliability issues that make it poor for emergency preparedness in 2025. Unlike Iridium's global coverage or Inmarsat's reliable 90% coverage, Globalstar works well only in certain regions. For life-saving emergency communication, choose Iridium or Inmarsat instead - when Hurricane Helene hit, Globalstar users experienced service failures while Iridium/Inmarsat users stayed connected.

What This Means

During Hurricane Helene in 2024, emergency responders reported that Globalstar devices experienced significant outages in affected areas, while Iridium and Inmarsat devices maintained reliable communication. A search and rescue team in North Carolina couldn't reach base via their Globalstar device but successfully coordinated rescue operations using an Iridium backup phone.

Globalstar operates with fewer satellites and has known coverage gaps, especially over oceans, polar regions, and parts of Africa and Asia. While it works adequately in North America and Europe where coverage is good, it's unreliable for global emergency preparedness.

For emergency communication where your life might depend on connectivity, Globalstar's limitations make it a risky choice. You want a satellite network that works everywhere, every time - not one that works only in certain regions or under ideal conditions.

How It Works

Globalstar Network Limitations (2025):

Technical Issues:

  • Fewer satellites than Iridium (24 vs 66 active satellites)
  • Aging satellite constellation with some satellites failing
  • Relies on ground stations that can fail during disasters
  • Coverage gaps over large ocean areas and remote regions
  • Inconsistent service quality even in covered areas

Coverage Comparison:

  • Iridium: 100% global coverage, works everywhere
  • Inmarsat: 90% global coverage, reliable where covered
  • Globalstar: ~80% coverage with significant gaps and reliability issues

Why experts recommend avoiding Globalstar for emergencies: Emergency communication requires absolute reliability. When your life depends on calling for help, you can't afford coverage gaps or network failures. Globalstar's track record shows too many service interruptions during critical moments.

Where It Works and Where It Doesn't

Globalstar works reasonably well in:

  • Continental United States (though with occasional gaps)
  • Canada (southern regions, unreliable in far north)
  • Europe (Western Europe better than Eastern)
  • Parts of South America (coverage varies significantly)
  • Some coastal areas (but not reliable offshore)

Globalstar has poor or no coverage:

  • Large ocean areas (Atlantic, Pacific, Indian Oceans)
  • Most of Africa and Asia
  • Polar regions (both Arctic and Antarctic)
  • Remote islands and territories
  • Areas experiencing satellite failures
  • Regions without local ground stations

Better alternatives for emergency preparedness: Choose Iridium for true global coverage or Inmarsat for excellent coverage with cost savings. Both have proven track records during major disasters when Globalstar experienced outages.

Pros and Cons

✅ Globalstar Advantages

  • Lower costs in some regions
  • Adequate coverage in parts of North America
  • Some data/messaging capabilities
  • Works for non-emergency communication
  • Easier to find used devices
  • Simple setup when working properly

❌ Globalstar Disadvantages

  • Significant coverage gaps globally
  • Unreliable during disasters
  • Aging satellite constellation
  • Depends on ground stations that can fail
  • Poor for emergency preparedness
  • Not suitable for global travel or remote areas

Who This Is Best For

Globalstar might work for:

  • Budget-conscious users in well-covered areas only
  • Non-emergency communication in North America/Europe
  • People who never travel to remote areas
  • Users wanting low-cost backup communication (with limitations)
  • Casual use where reliability isn't critical

Choose Iridium or Inmarsat instead if you need:

  • Reliable emergency communication
  • Global travel capabilities
  • Life-saving communication during disasters
  • Professional or business-critical communication
  • Coverage in remote areas or over oceans
  • Communication you can depend on when it matters most

Expert recommendation: For emergency preparedness, invest in Iridium or Inmarsat. The cost difference isn't worth the risk of Globalstar failing when you need communication most. Emergency responders, search and rescue teams, and emergency preparedness experts consistently recommend Iridium or Inmarsat over Globalstar.

Mini-FAQ

Has Globalstar improved recently?

Globalstar has made some improvements, but fundamental issues remain: fewer satellites, coverage gaps, and reliability problems during disasters. While adequate for casual use in covered areas, it still can't match Iridium or Inmarsat for emergency preparedness.

Why do some people still use Globalstar?

Lower costs and adequate coverage in specific regions where they live/work. However, most emergency preparedness experts recommend paying more for Iridium or Inmarsat to ensure reliable life-saving communication when needed.

Should I consider Globalstar to save money?

Not for emergency preparedness. The money saved isn't worth the risk of failed communication during a life-threatening emergency. Consider it false economy - like buying a cheap fire extinguisher that might not work when your house burns down.

Summary / Takeaway

Globalstar is not a good satellite network for emergency preparedness in 2025 due to coverage gaps, reliability issues, and service failures during disasters. While it works adequately in some regions for non-critical communication, it can't be trusted for life-saving emergency communication.

During Hurricane Helene and other major disasters, Globalstar experienced outages while Iridium and Inmarsat maintained reliable service. Emergency responders consistently recommend Iridium for global coverage or Inmarsat for cost-effective reliability over Globalstar.

For emergency preparedness where your life might depend on communication, choose proven networks: Iridium for maximum global coverage or Inmarsat for excellent coverage with cost savings. Don't risk your safety on Globalstar's unreliable network.
